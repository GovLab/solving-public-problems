The primacy of technology in our daily lives combined with the urgent need to design and implement solutions to public problems require a new curriculum of public entrepreneurship.
This transdisciplinary class offers a systematic introduction to the problem-solving skills you will need to take a mission driven project from idea to implementation.

By combining the teaching of quantitative and qualitative methods with participatory and equitable approaches that include the communities we aim to help in the problem-solving process, this course will give you the tools to design and implement powerful solutions to contemporary challenges.

Learning Objectives

  1. Introduce you to leading social and public innovators who are passionate about real world change.
  2. Give you methods and tools — new ways of working — to become a catalyst for change.
  3. Introduce you to the latest tools and technologies for designing and implementing mission-driven projects.
  4. Ensure that you know how to use big data, collective intelligence and behavioral insights to solve problems more efficiently and effectively.
  1. Ensure you have the ability to transparently and democratically, working with communities, not just for them.
  2. Teach you to build powerful partnerships that yield high-quality results.
  3. Help you learn to combine subject matter expertise and problem solving skills to address real world problems.
  4. Introduce you to careers in the private sector, public sector and nonprofit sector where you can make a difference and earn a living.
